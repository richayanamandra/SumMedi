# Literature Review: Medical Question Answering on the MedQA Dataset

| Name of Paper & Link | Date of Publishing | Objective | Methodology | Results |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
|([https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.10341](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.10341)) | 2022 | To propose a domain-specific generative model pre-trained on large-scale biomedical literature to overcome the generation limits of discriminative models. [1] | A GPT-style autoregressive language model pre-trained exclusively on PubMed abstracts and full texts. [1] | Achieved a 50.3% accuracy on the MedQA dataset.[2] Outperformed previous models on most tasks, setting a new record on PubMedQA (78.2%) and demonstrating strong biomedical text generation abilities. [1] |
|([https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.09617](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.09617)) | 2023 | To bridge the performance gap between AI and human clinicians in medical QA by leveraging base model improvements and clinical fine-tuning. [3] | Utilized the PaLM 2 base model (Med-PaLM 2) coupled with medical domain instruction fine-tuning and a novel ensemble refinement (ER) prompting approach. [3] | Achieved an 86.5% accuracy on MedQA. It was the first model to reach expert-level performance, improving upon its predecessor by over 19% and setting a new state-of-the-art at the time. [3] |
|([https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.16079](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.16079)) | 2023 | To democratize access to large-scale medical LLMs by releasing an open-source suite adapted specifically to the medical domain. [4] | Built on Llama-2 (7B and 70B) using distributed training, with continuous pre-training on a massive medical corpus (GAP-REPLAY) and evaluated using Self-Consistency Chain-of-Thought. [4] | Achieved 70.2% on the 4-option MedQA and 64.4% on the 5-option variant.[4] Brought open-source capabilities much closer to proprietary models, outperforming GPT-3.5 on medical benchmarks and providing accessible weights. [4] |
| [AlpaCare: Instruction-tuned large language models for medical application](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.14558) | 2023 | To instruction-tune LLMs using clinician-crafted data to ensure clinical soundness, safety, and real-world applicability. [5] | Fine-tuned LLaMA-based architectures (7B and 13B) on the specialized MedInstruct-52k dataset. [6] | Scored 40.6% on MedQA in baseline zero-shot settings [5], though it hit 91.7% on factual safety evaluations.[7] Showcased robust capability in following complex medical instructions safely, highlighting a trade-off between raw multiple-choice accuracy and clinical harmlessness. [7] |
|([https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.10373](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.10373)) | 2024 | To create and multilingually evaluate a collection of open-source medical models based on the highly efficient Mistral architecture. [8] | Mistral 7B was further pre-trained on PubMed Central text, followed by supervised fine-tuning and advanced model merging (e.g., DARE, SLERP). [9] | Achieved a peak accuracy of 51.1% on MedQA using the DARE merge strategy.[9] Proved that lightweight 7B models can be highly competitive when merged, and conducted one of the first large-scale multilingual evaluations in the medical domain. [8, 9] |
| [Capabilities of Gemini Models in Medicine](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.18416) | 2024 | To evaluate Gemini models in medicine and introduce the specialized Med-Gemini multimodal family for advanced clinical reasoning. [10] | Fine-tuned Gemini 1.0 and 1.5 Pro models utilizing a novel uncertainty-guided web search strategy to dynamically retrieve external information. [11] | Achieved an outstanding 91.1% accuracy on MedQA.[11] Surpassed GPT-4 performance across multiple benchmarks and demonstrated exceptional long-context retrieval from health records alongside real-world multimodal clinical utility. [10, 11] |
| [Aloe: A Family of Fine-tuned Open Healthcare LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.01886) | 2024 | To develop a robust, open suite of specialized healthcare LLMs with strong context retrieval capabilities. | Applied large-scale supervised instruction tuning and model merging to Llama 3.1 and Qwen base architectures. [12, 13] | The Aloe Beta 70B variant achieved 64.47% accuracy on MedQA.[12] Provided a highly competitive open-source family of medical models with strong ethical alignment and context retrieval for clinical QA. |
|([https://www.researchgate.net/publication/381319073_Med42_-Evaluating_Fine-Tuning_Strategies_for_Medical_LLMs_Full-Parameter_vs_Parameter-Efficient_Approaches](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/381319073_Med42_-Evaluating_Fine-Tuning_Strategies_for_Medical_LLMs_Full-Parameter_vs_Parameter-Efficient_Approaches)) | 2024 | To systematically evaluate the effectiveness of fine-tuning strategies (full-parameter vs. parameter-efficient) across medical domains. [14] | Developed and refined a series of LLMs based on the Llama-2 architecture, specifically designed to enhance medical knowledge retrieval and reasoning. [14] | The Med42 model showed an accuracy level of 72.0% on the US Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE) datasets, setting a new standard in performance for openly available medical LLMs. [14] |
|([https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.03590](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.03590)) | 2024 | To systematically compare the impact of run-time steering strategies versus intrinsic reinforcement learning reasoning on medical problem-solving. [15] | Evaluated GPT-4 and GPT-4o paired with Medprompt (dynamic CoT and ensemble refinement) against the inference-time compute architecture of OpenAI o1-preview. | GPT-4 with Medprompt reached 90.2%, while the o1-preview model achieved 96.0%.[15, 16] Highlighted a Pareto frontier between cost and accuracy, proving that while o1 sets new accuracy records autonomously, GPT-4o with Medprompt remains a highly cost-effective alternative. [15, 16] |
|([https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.00016](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.00016)) | 2025 | To rigorously assess the medical reasoning capabilities and clinical alignment of DeepSeek R1 compared to human expertise. | Evaluated the 671B parameter DeepSeek R1, a mixture-of-experts model trained heavily via reinforcement learning to generate explicit reasoning traces (Chain-of-Thought). [17] | Achieved 93% diagnostic accuracy on the subset of 100 MedQA clinical cases analyzed. Proved that inference-time reasoning greatly improves diagnostic capabilities, though it also revealed persistent human-like cognitive errors such as anchoring bias and overthinking. |
|([https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17387](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17387)) | 2025 | To enhance verifiable medical reasoning in a 32B parameter LLM and explore its potential for practical deployment in healthcare IT. | Employed a multi-stage training pipeline combining supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and reinforcement learning (RL) using curated Long Chain-of-Thought datasets and an evidence-based diagnostic chain simulation. | Reached an 87.1% accuracy on the MedQA-USMLE dataset. Demonstrated that applying RL, even with a limited dataset, significantly boosts medical reasoning and creates more trustworthy models for real-world clinical workflows. |
